The February 2024 Pikabot distribution campaign began with spearphishing emails containing hyperlinks to malicious ZIP archives. Upon execution, obfuscated JavaScript files initiated PowerShell scripts to download and install Pikabot. Attackers hijacked the execution flow by tampering with the legitimate grepWinNP3.exe tool, modifying it to load malicious code. This multi-stage deployment prioritized stealth, transitioning from script-based delivery to memory-resident operations.
